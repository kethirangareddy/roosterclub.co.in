How to avoid scams when buying roosters & birds online

Rooster Club knowledge series · ~7 min read

Buying birds over WhatsApp groups and random Facebook pages is convenient — and risky. Every breeder has heard the story: a beautiful Aseel cock at a great price, money sent in advance, and then the seller vanishes. The good news is that almost every poultry scam follows the same handful of patterns. Learn them once and you'll spot them instantly.

The five most common scams

1. The advance-payment disappearance

The classic. A seller insists on full payment before you see the bird, often with urgency ("another buyer is coming, send now to hold it"). Once paid, they block you. Rule: never pay in full for a bird you haven't inspected. A genuine seller is happy to let you see the bird first.

2. Stolen or fake photos

Scammers lift champion-bird photos from other pages and present them as their own stock. If the photos look like studio or show shots while the price is suspiciously low, be suspicious. Ask for a fresh photo or video of the exact bird holding today's newspaper or a written note — scammers can't produce it.

3. The fake breeder identity

A brand-new account, no history, no reviews, a borrowed farm name. Reputation is the scammer's weakest point because it can't be faked overnight. Always check how long the seller has been active and what others say about them.

4. The transport-deposit trick

After you agree, a "courier" or "transport agent" demands a separate refundable deposit for a special crate, vaccination certificate, or permit. It's all invented. Legitimate transport is arranged transparently and paid on delivery, not via mystery deposits.

5. The overpayment / refund scam

A "buyer" sends a screenshot showing they paid too much and asks you to refund the difference. The original payment never clears. Only trust money you can confirm has actually arrived in your account — never a screenshot.

Your safety checklist

  • Inspect before paying — in person, or at minimum on a live video call where you control what's shown.
  • Check reputation — prefer sellers with verified identity and a real review history.
  • Keep a record — agree details in writing and confirm a deal receipt both sides can see.
  • Be wary of urgency — pressure to "pay now" is the oldest trick in the book.
  • Pay traceably, on delivery — avoid irreversible advance transfers to strangers.
  • Trust your gut — if a deal feels too good, it usually is.
The single best protection is buying from people whose identity and track record you can actually see.

Why a trusted marketplace changes the game

Scams thrive in places with no identity, no reputation and no record — exactly the conditions of an open WhatsApp group. A purpose-built marketplace flips that: sellers can verify their identity with KYC, every deal can be confirmed with a receipt, reviews follow a seller around, and bad actors can be reported and removed. That's the whole reason Rooster Club exists — to take the poultry trade out of the shadows and make honest dealing the easy default.

If you've been targeted by online fraud, report it to your bank immediately and to your local cyber-crime helpline. Acting fast gives the best chance of recovering funds.
